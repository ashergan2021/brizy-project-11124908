Growing Pains

Triston & Jayden


Being a teenager is an unavoidable part of our lives. During this phase, they are constantly learning and exploring new things about life. However, not everything is of course sunshine and rainbows. Teenagers in this phase tend to have a change in the brain and body, which affects the way they feel, think and behave. 


There are several changes in the body during adolescence which is from 9 and a half years to 14 years old. Your hips will widen and your body will get curvier. You will grow taller. Acne - You may develop acne. This is a condition of the skin that shows up as bumps – most commonly on the face, neck, shoulders, upper back and chest. Your private parts may start to change, depending on your gender. There will also be a change in your voice, which makes your voice deeper. You'll get hair under your arms, in your pubic area, and on your legs. 


As a result of this process, we tend to be more inclined to feelings such as emotions, impulses, aggression and instinctive behavior due to the use of their amygdala, the central cortex of the brain. Therefore, teenagers will be more irrational or easily agitated as their body tries to cope with hormonal change and growth. Teenagers may also be more rebellious during this stage as their brain develops and want control of the situation at hand.


How do I beat all these problems? Think positive. Looking different from your friends can be hard on your self-esteem. Instead of seeing your changing body as a bad thing, embrace your new curves. When you look in the mirror, learn to love the new you. You can also talk to an adult. Tell a trusted adult – such as your mom, an aunt, or your school nurse – about what's bothering you. Your friends – not so much. They might mean well, but you really want someone who knows more and has been there, done that.


In conclusion, you should be able to cope with your body changes and live life as per normal. Do remember to stay calm, as at times you are more inclined to feelings, and when you are angry, leave the scene, or take big breaths. Do not be too angry, instead, widen your perspective and think about how other people would feel. Stay strong, and get through this stage. Good luck!



Growing Pains

Caden & Regan


How would puberty affect a child’s brain?


Researchers have learned, however, that puberty affects not just your body but also your brain. This is due to hormonal changes that connect to brain cells and alter how the brain learns and grows throughout puberty. These alterations are beneficial since they aid in the development of the brain for new types of learning.


How will puberty affect a child’s body?

For females, first signs of puberty are 1 or 2 years before an obvious growth spurt occurs. As she develops the features of a woman, her body will start to accumulate fat, notably in her breasts and around her hips and thighs. Her limbs will all grow in size as well.

The males would first experience an expansion of the scrotum and testes during puberty, during this time, the penis would not expand. The penis would develop in tandem with the testes and scrotum. Pubic hair would start growing in an area surrounding the genitals and would be long and silky.


How would puberty affect relationships?

Relationships between family members are often reorganized. The teens would desire more independence and emotional distance from their parents and would focus more attention towards social interaction and friendships. The friends can be of the same gender, same gender in groups or even friends of other genders. Early pubertal maturation, particularly in females, might result in a higher chance of love relationships before they have the psychological maturity and interpersonal skills needed to manga partnerships successfully.



How would puberty affect a child’s emotions?

The child’s emotions may get stronger and more powerful during this period of time. They would have more exaggerated mood swings that will shift quickly and frequently. They may be experiencing significant emotions for the first time. It is very common for the children to be perplexed, terrified and furious for no reason.


How does puberty affect a child’s self-esteem?

Changes in puberty can affect how a child feels about themselves. Women would feel self-conscious and even embarrassed about the changes in their body. Guys would feel that they aren’t tall, muscular or strong enough, on the other hand, their female counterparts would feel pressured to be skinny.


How would puberty affect a child’s mental health?

Early puberty may increase the chances or risks of mental health problems in individuals. Anxiety, depression, eating disorders, conduct disorders, attention deficit and hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and self-harm are very common in teens.

A brain, a clump of soft tissue, including grey and white matter, weighing 1.5kg, is the most powerful organ in our whole body. Our brain controls thought, memory, emotion, touch, motor skills, vision and many other functions. Sometimes, you may feel that it is hard to control your feelings and emotions, especially for teenagers going through the adolescent stage. So, why does that happen?

Adolescence is a time of significant growth and development inside the teenage brain. Scientists have identified a specific region of the brain called the amygdala responsible for immediate reactions, including fear and aggressive behaviour, which develops early. The part of the brain which changes the most during adolescence is the prefrontal cortex, which is involved in a range of cognitive functions such as decision making, planning, and inhibiting inappropriate behaviour, such that you can prevent yourself from doing something wrong or saying something rude; however, the prefrontal cortex develops later, and would still be changing and maturing well into adulthood. This would be one of the reasons why teenagers going through adolescence may have a harder time controlling their actions, feelings and emotions.

Firstly, it could be due to the type of food one consumes. Different types of food change your mood respectively. Oats, grains, and legumes have slower glucose release, allowing for a steadier attentiveness level. A high glycemic food such as bread causes a rapid release of glucose into the bloodstream, and after a while, the glucose is fully used up. One’s blood pressure shoots down, and with it, our attention span decreases. The amount of sugar and fibre subgroups in the body affects how the body and brain respond.
